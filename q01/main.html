Our History
It was in the year 1899, on the 13th day of November , when the Bengal Nagpur Railway laid the foundation of this school. The name then was BNR European School and as the name suggests the gates were opened only to the children of the ruling European community. It was later that the Anglo Indian Community was allowed to send their children. The admissions of Indians became possible only after the end of Indiaâ€™s epic fight for freedom. In the year 1943, the school was raised to the status of a High School preparing Students for the Senior Cambridge Examination. After Independence the school was renamed and was known as Anglo-Indian school till 1959.

Our Vision
The South Eastern Railway mixed Higher Secondary School (E.M) aims to be an institution of excellence, dedicated to producing a strong secular leaders of the future.

Our Mission
An awareness of the environment, A concern for human rights and democratic values, Provides an environment in which every student discovers and realizes his full potential.
